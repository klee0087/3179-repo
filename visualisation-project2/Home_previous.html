How suicide claimed the lives of 6,732,817 people

Suicide cases across the globe

Suicide is the act is taking one's own life voluntarily. Many complex frameworks of internal and external factors usually contribute to this act (Lewitzka, Denzin, Sauer, Bauer, & Jabs, 2016). Such risk factors could be due to mental illnesses, trauma, usage of substances (drugs and alcohol), hopelessness, attempted suicide and more (verywellmind, 2015). 

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), it is said that more than 700,000 people die due to suicide every year. For every suicide there are many more people who attempt suicide (single most important risk factor) . Around 77% of global suicides are from low- and middle-income countries (World Health Organization, 2021).

Average suicide cases by continent over the years

According to Wikipedia and Euronews, Europe is the most suicidal region in the world . In 2015, out of the 5.2 million deaths reported in the European Union, 56200 (1.1%) were due to intentional self-harm (Eurostat, 2018). Studies have shown factors such as socialist backgrounds, rapid socio-political and economic changes in integrating into the European Union, high alcohol consumption and abuse among victims of suicide were likely the causes for high suicide rates (Varnik et al., 2008).

The heatmap has shown that suicide cases have been decreasing in the later years. Each country have been putting efforts in reducing suicide rates such as improving access to mental health treatments, community interactions (such as suicide hotlines), suicide prevention across healthcare, social, education, employment services and in the workplace (Weir, 2019).

Average suicide cases by country

Lithuania has the highest average number of suicide cases from 1985 and 2015. Factors such as depressive weather, hard living conditions, radical changes after the collapse of the Soviet Union and alcoholism contributed to high suicide rates (Asmonaitis, 2017).

The Russian Federation has the second-highest average number of suicide cases from 1985 and 2015. The dissolution of the Soviet Union, market reforms, the First Chechen War, high alcohol consumption and reduced income has led to an increase in suicide cases (Wikipedia Contributors, 2021).
